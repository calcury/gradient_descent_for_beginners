Principle: Descend Along the Steepest Direction

The gradient (\(\nabla L\)) points in the direction of the steepest increase of the function. To minimize the loss, we move in the opposite direction of the gradient.

Update Formula:

\(k_{new} = k_{old} - \alpha \cdot \frac{\partial L}{\partial k}\)
\(b_{new} = b_{old} - \alpha \cdot \frac{\partial L}{\partial b}\)

Where \(\alpha\) is the learning rate (step size).
